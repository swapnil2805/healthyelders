Follow a heart-healthy diet.

Low in calories but high in vitamins, minerals, and fiber, colorful fruits and vegetables are great for your heart.

Seniors should get at least five servings per day of these nutrition all-stars. Read all nutrition labels and limit saturated and trans fats and added sugars.

Buy plenty of nuts and high-fiber foods. Avoid high-fat dairy or meats and limit the amount of alcohol you drink.

Quit smoking.

Smoking is a leading cause of preventable death. It can also raise your risk of heart disease and heart attack and worsen already existing heart disease risk factors.

Smoking damages the artery walls, but quitting—even later in life—can lower your risk of heart disease, stroke, and cancer over time.

f you’re struggling to quit, chat with your doctor about programs available to you or consider joining a local support group.

Aging at Home

Learn a new skill

Learning something new is not only fun, but it can also help stimulate the brain.

Studies have shown that older adults who learned a new skill had more memory improvement than those who only socialized or did less cognitive-related activities.

Something as simple as opening a cookbook you’ve had for years and making a new recipe can help build confidence and keep your mind sharp.

Improve your sleep schedule

Studies have shown that both insufficient sleep and sleeping longer than average can increase your likelihood of developing dementia.

It is recommended that people over the age of 65 get seven to eight hours of sleep per night.

if you’re experiencing extreme tiredness or any symptoms that are preventing you from sleeping consistently, consult with your doctor.

Keeping your Mind Active

    Engage in Lifelong Learning

  1. Stay mentally active by engaging in lifelong learning. Pursue activities that challenge the mind, such as learning a new language, taking up a new hobby, or enrolling in educational courses. Continuous learning helps stimulate the brain and promotes cognitive health.
  2. Foster strong social connections and maintain emotional support networks. Regular interaction with friends, family, and community helps combat feelings of isolation, promotes emotional well-being, and provides opportunities for stimulating conversations.
  3. Incorporate mindfulness practices and stress management techniques into daily life. Activities like meditation, deep breathing exercises, or yoga can help reduce stress levels, enhance emotional resilience, and contribute to overall mental well-being.
  4. Cultivate a positive attitude and practice gratitude. Focus on positive aspects of life, express gratitude for small joys, and adopt an optimistic outlook. A positive mindset is associated with improved mental health, coping abilities, and a sense of fulfillment.
  5. Engage in brain-boosting activities to enhance cognitive function. This includes puzzles, games, reading, and other activities that challenge the mind. Such activities promote neural connections, maintain mental sharpness, and contribute to cognitive resilience.
